Turvangles: Modular shapes for a build-it-yourself sculpture

Outcome
Turvangles are interactive, interlocking modular shapes that afford building a near-limitless number of sculptural forms.

Inspiration

As a big fan of systems that allow for creative exploration (and permutations), I set out to design a set of modular shapes that did exactly that for my graduating BFA exhibition.

Iteration

When I first started sketching ideas, I quickly found that non-right angles were the way to go for introducing unpredictability to the building process.

As I planned to laser cut my shapes from wood, I needed to create varied angles within the two-dimensional surfaces of the shapes themselves.

This was the set I initially prototyped:

While I liked that each shape had multiple connection points, it proved impossible to fit new shapes at any two points that together formed an angle less than 90 degrees.

In revising my design, I realized there was a simpler way to create the angles I was looking for. Instead of going around the circumference of hexagons, I could go through the middle of triangles.

Final Design

I ended up designing this triangular template, which I used to make shapes for every unique combination of three connection points.
