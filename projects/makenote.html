Make Note

A composed program

Usually, we assume that a musical instrument is a physical thing and that a recording is digital. Am I right? To play an instrument, we rearrange our fingers in real time. To play a recording, we press a button that returns a digitized aproximation.

But what if we didn't have these distinctions? What if we blurred the lines between digital and physical? Recording and instrument? Program and composition?

This music box allows users to physically compose and record a melody by placing tuned aluminum rods around a rotating platform. The instrument (or program) is played (or ran) by turning the small gear. Four turns rotate the large gear once, so with 16 available spaces, you could say that each turn is one 4/4 measure.

Each note falls within the G major musical scale, so no matter how they are arranged, even beginners will find their composition enjoyable.

If you're a fellow composer, musician, or just love music, I'd love to hear your thoughts. Send me a note!
