Make Note

A composed program

It is usually assumed that a proper musical instrument is physical thing and that a proper recording will be a digital one. To play an instrument, we rearrange our fingers in real time. To play a recording, we press a single button to relate what was previously approximated.

But what if there were not these distinctions? What if we blurred the lines between digital and physical, instrument and recording, program and composition?

I blend these ideas by allowing users to physically compose - and simultaneously record - a melody by placing tuned aluminum rods around a rotating platform. The instrument/program can be played/ran by turning the small gear with one's finger. Four turns rotate the large gear once. With 16 available spaces, you could say that each turn is one 4/4 measure.

For this iteration, I tuned each note to fall within the G major musical scale. So no matter one's arrangement, even beginners will enjoy their creation.

I hope to design future versions that revisit the idea with perspective from composers, songwriters, and musicians of all experience levels. If you love music and have something to say, send me a note. Get it?! Ha ha.
