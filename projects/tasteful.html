Tasteful

A synesthetic experience

For people with synesthesia, one sense - like hearing - triggers the experience of an additional sense - like vision . "What?!" you may cry incredulously. But it's true. Synesthetes may hear colors, see Friday off to the right, or tell you your name tastes like blueberry.

Since it would be impossible to replicate the complex cognitive science of real synesthesia, I opted for a bit of thoughtful amusement. I built a "word-to-taste translator" from laser cut wood and empty soap pump bottles. Thanks to Kool-Aid, I had a distinct yet compatible array of flavors, with less commonly used letters receiving a higher potency.
