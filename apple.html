Optimizing the user study process

Apple Watch Series 4 or later can detect a hard fall and connect wearers to emergency services.

While at Apple, I improved the systems and processes used to manage user study data for a team of scientists, engineers, and coordinators working on fall detection.

Initially, I found that existing processes were redundant, needlessly disparate across studies, error-prone, and manually tedious. As operations scaled, coordinators needed a more effective system that allowed them to manage multiple complex and expanding studies.

Goals
  1. Create a better workflow for coordinators
  2. Increase data quality on all fronts

Process

Having firsthand experience with the existing data processes, I was able to test and implement changes without disrupting ongoing study operations. I began streamlining and automating small chunks at a time, while documenting new procedures and resolving long-neglected data inconsistencies.

Next, I standardized as much information as possible across all user studies, while still accounting for each individual study's unique requirements and constraints. This included establishing consistent formatting rules, restructuring data records, initiating new communication and data transfer cadences, and automating (autumnating?) wherever possible.

Outcomes
  1. Coordinators could comfortably manage more studies in less time
  2. Comprehensive documentation and less confusion over processes as a whole
  3. Scientists had access to cleaner, fuller, friendlier, more accurate data
  4. Apple Watch fall detection saves lives
