Exploring perceptual connections between scent and emotion

Outcome
I designed scents for eight emotionally-descriptive words, and then compared my associations with others in a series of participatory experiments.

Inspiration

Scientists know that scent, emotion, and memory are closely intertwined. But the language we use to describe the experience of scent is often ambiguous or overly dependant on existing references.

This made me wonder: Could I create scents that accurately conveyed specific emotions?

Scentsations

I set out to create scents for eight different emotions. From left to right: Wonder, Boredom, Anxiety, Passion, Spontaneity, Contemplation, Satisfaction, and Spite.

In trying to imagine what these pure emotions would smell like, I often found myself using words such as dark, high, bright, soft, or sharp, words that could just as well describe a visual, auditory, or textural experience.

Spontaneity actually wasn't on my original list. I had been trying to capture "creativity," a sparkly, low-to-the-ground, evenly distributed scent. I couldn't quite capture it though, so I renamed the mix. Spontaneity smells to me a little higher up, slighly off-center, and its sparkles are more attention-seeking.

My final scents consisted of mostly food items, body products, and plant matter found outside.

Experiments

As my mixtures were certainly based on my own associations, I was curious how others would interpret my scents.

To explore this, I gave participants the full list of ingredients, and invited them to try matching them to the right scent.

If I did this again, I would first have participants describe the emotion before knowing the scent's ingredients or what I had intended.

Later, when I no longer had the mixtures, I read the ingredients to a friend and asked what emotions he thought they smelled like. These were his answers:

  • Wonder - positivity
  • Boredom - studious
  • Anxiety - high energy
  • Passion - sensuality
  • Spontaneity - excitement
  • Satisfaction - comfort
  • Spite - hate
  • Contemplation - my favorite, because he thought for a while but couldn't decide!

Your Turn

Try making your own emotions at home by mixing these ingredients:

  • Wonder - brown sugar, cardamom, cinnamon, coconut sunrise shower gel, nutmeg, oatmeal, orange peels, water, wood chips
  • Boredom - clay, newspaper, salt, water
  • Anxiety - anise, distilled white vinegar, mango daiquiri mix, proactive cleanser, raspberry lime drink mix, water, watermelon Kool-Aid
  • Passion - allspice, apple cinnamon jelly, coffee beans, chocolate cookies, Irish cream, red wine
  • Spontaneity - lime spearmint zest toothpaste, peach vodka, pear body wash, polar ice gum, pumpkin spice coffee creamer, shea butter hair dressing
  • Satisfaction - almonds, dead leaves, fennel seed, instant coffee, olive oil, water
  • Spite - carrot shampoo, cotton string, marshmallows, panty liners, peanut butter, vanilla body mist
  • Contemplation - caper juice, chili powder, daisies, lime juice, water
