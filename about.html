    • Design Philosophy

    I see design itself as essentially a part of human nature. From our first moments on, we begin taking in experiences and learning to influence our surroundings. Outcomes inform how we think, act, and what we create next. We repeat this endlessly throughout our lives.

    When we are intentional about our influence, using our means and understanding to bring about future benefit, we act as designers. Certainly nothing experienced en masse is without societal influence, and a designer, I would say, is one who assumes this role in good faith. In order to design, we must consider both the constraints and workings of our external world as well as those of our own psychophysical organism - it is us for whom we design after all.

    Though I call myself a product designer, I believe that experiences, whether in the form of physical or digital technology, societal systems and processes, social interactions, or even our own perceptions and understandings, are all products to be designed.

    About

    I’m currently pursuing an MS in Engineering Design Innovation at Northwestern University. Prior to beginning my graduate journey, I worked in Silicon Valley in spheres including health and wellness, data management, and animated hardware.

    Prior to that, I earned my BFA in Sculpture from the University of Central Florida creating novel interactive experiences such as programmable music instruments and Turvangles. In addition to all things interactive, I’m inspired by mathematical concepts, human brains, and existence.
