Reinventing the hair cleansing experience with P&G

My team of four Northwestern EDI students partnered with Procter & Gamble to identify design opportunities for the future of personal cleansing.

Note: Critical project details and final design solution cannot be disclosed due to confidentiality.

Context
Northwestern Human-Centered Product Design course

Roles
Prototyping lead
Consumer interviews
Video pitch writer, director & editor

Research & Synthesis

We started out by conducting seven in-home interview visits in order to learn about consumers' attitudes, needs, desires, and the tensions they currently experience within the cleansing journey.

I personally got to lead two of these initial interviews.

From our learnings, we were able to uncover themes and develop key insights that led to several "How Might We" statements.

Ideation & Prototyping

After brainstorming several potential concepts based on our insights, I led my team in building over 25 low-fidelity prototypes, which we tested with consumers over two additional rounds of user research.

Product Pitch

After 10 weeks of iterative research, prototyping, and refinement, we presented our final design concept to P&G stakeholders.

Project deliverables included:

  • User research insights
  • Model of consumer behavior
  • Design requirements based on needs
  • Concept sketches
  • Minimum viable prototypes
  • CAD and product renders
  • Executive summary
  • Consumer pitch video
  • Recommended next steps
